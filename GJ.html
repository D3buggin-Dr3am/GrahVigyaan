Category:GJ 3470 b is a sub-Neptune gas giant smaller than Jupiter, with a significant atmosphere likely made of hydrogen and helium.
Distance from Earth: The planet is approximately 99 light-years away in the constellation Aries.
Host Star Name: GJ 3470 b orbits the star GJ 3470, a cool red dwarf star much dimmer than our Sun.
Orbital Period: This exoplanet completes its orbit in about 7.8 Earth days, indicating a close distance to its host star.
Surface Conditions: The surface conditions are still largely unknown, but it likely has a thick atmosphere that retains heat.
